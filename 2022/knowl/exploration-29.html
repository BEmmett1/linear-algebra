Exploration 3.1.12.

If \(V,W\) are vectors spaces, with associated zero vectors \(\vec{0}_V\) and \(\vec{0}_W\text{,}\) and \(T:V \rightarrow W\) is a linear transformation, does \(T(\vec{0}_V) = \vec{0}_W\text{?}\) Prove this is true, or find a counterexample.Assume \(f: V \rightarrow W\) is a linear transformation between vector spaces. Let \(\vec{v} \in V\) with additive inverse \(\vec{v}^{-1}\text{.}\) Prove that \(f(\vec{v}^{-1}) = [f(\vec{v})]^{-1}\text{.}\)
