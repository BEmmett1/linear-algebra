1. Find the standard matrix for the linear transformation \(S:\mathbb{R}^ 3 \to \mathbb{R}^ 4 \) given by

    \[S\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} x \\ y \\ z \end{array}\right] \right) = \left[\begin{array}{c} -x + 3 \, y + 4 \, z \\ -x - z \\ 2 \, x - 2 \, y - z \\ -3 \, x + y - 6 \, z \end{array}\right] .\]

  2. Let \(T:\mathbb{R}^ 4 \to \mathbb{R}^ 2 \) be the linear transformation given by the standard matrix

    \[ \left[\begin{array}{cccc} 2 & 6 & 1 & -3 \\ 1 & 3 & 1 & -2 \end{array}\right] .\]

    Compute \(T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -7 \\ 6 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] \right)\).

Answer:

  1. \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 3 & 4 \\ -1 & 0 & -1 \\ 2 & -2 & -1 \\ -3 & 1 & -6 \end{array}\right] \]

  2. \[T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -7 \\ 6 \\ -5 \end{array}\right] \right)= \left[\begin{array}{c} -27 \\ -8 \end{array}\right] \]
