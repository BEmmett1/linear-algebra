1. Find the standard matrix for the linear transformation \(S:\mathbb{R}^ 3 \to \mathbb{R}^ 3 \) given by

    \[S\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{array}\right] \right) = \left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \, x_{1} - 5 \, x_{2} + 7 \, x_{3} \\ x_{1} + 4 \, x_{2} - 4 \, x_{3} \\ -3 \, x_{1} - 4 \, x_{2} + 5 \, x_{3} \end{array}\right] .\]

  2. Let \(T:\mathbb{R}^ 3 \to \mathbb{R}^ 2 \) be the linear transformation given by the standard matrix

    \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc} -1 & 6 & 0 \\ -1 & 5 & 0 \end{array}\right] .\]

    Compute \(T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -8 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] \right)\).

Answer:

  1. \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc} -5 & -5 & 7 \\ 1 & 4 & -4 \\ -3 & -4 & 5 \end{array}\right] \]

  2. \[T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -8 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] \right)= \left[\begin{array}{c} 32 \\ 28 \end{array}\right] \]
