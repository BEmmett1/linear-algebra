1. Find the standard matrix for the linear transformation \(S:\mathbb{R}^ 2 \to \mathbb{R}^ 4 \) given by

    \[S\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} x \\ y \end{array}\right] \right) = \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \, x + 7 \, y \\ 2 \, x - 5 \, y \\ -3 \, x + 4 \, y \\ -2 \, x + 6 \, y \end{array}\right] .\]

  2. Let \(T:\mathbb{R}^ 3 \to \mathbb{R}^ 3 \) be the linear transformation given by the standard matrix

    \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0 & 0 & -1 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 6 \end{array}\right] .\]

    Compute \(T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \right)\).

Answer:

  1. \[ \left[\begin{array}{cc} -3 & 7 \\ 2 & -5 \\ -3 & 4 \\ -2 & 6 \end{array}\right] \]

  2. \[T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \right)= \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 4 \\ -2 \end{array}\right] \]
