1. Find the standard matrix for the linear transformation \(S:\mathbb{R}^ 2 \to \mathbb{R}^ 3 \) given by

    \[S\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \end{array}\right] \right) = \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \, x_{1} - 5 \, x_{2} \\ x_{1} - 2 \, x_{2} \\ -2 \, x_{1} + x_{2} \end{array}\right] .\]

  2. Let \(T:\mathbb{R}^ 4 \to \mathbb{R}^ 1 \) be the linear transformation given by the standard matrix

    \[ \left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & 1 & 2 & -3 \end{array}\right] .\]

    Compute \(T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -8 \\ 3 \\ -6 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \right)\).

Answer:

  1. \[ \left[\begin{array}{cc} 2 & -5 \\ 1 & -2 \\ -2 & 1 \end{array}\right] \]

  2. \[T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -8 \\ 3 \\ -6 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \right)= \left[\begin{array}{c} -17 \end{array}\right] \]
