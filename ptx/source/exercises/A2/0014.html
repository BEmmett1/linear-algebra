1. Find the standard matrix for the linear transformation \(S:\mathbb{R}^ 2 \to \mathbb{R}^ 3 \) given by

    \[S\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} x \\ y \end{array}\right] \right) = \left[\begin{array}{c} x - 3 \, y \\ y \\ -2 \, y \end{array}\right] .\]

  2. Let \(T:\mathbb{R}^ 3 \to \mathbb{R}^ 2 \) be the linear transformation given by the standard matrix

    \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0 & -1 & -2 \\ 1 & 3 & 6 \end{array}\right] .\]

    Compute \(T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ 0 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] \right)\).

Answer:

  1. \[ \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -3 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 0 & -2 \end{array}\right] \]

  2. \[T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} 4 \\ 0 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] \right)= \left[\begin{array}{c} -14 \\ 46 \end{array}\right] \]
