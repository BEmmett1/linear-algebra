1. Find the standard matrix for the linear transformation \(S:\mathbb{R}^ 2 \to \mathbb{R}^ 3 \) given by

    \[S\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} x \\ y \end{array}\right] \right) = \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \, x + 5 \, y \\ -x - 4 \, y \\ x \end{array}\right] .\]

  2. Let \(T:\mathbb{R}^ 3 \to \mathbb{R}^ 4 \) be the linear transformation given by the standard matrix

    \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 2 & -7 & 8 \\ 0 & 1 & -3 \\ 0 & -2 & 7 \\ -1 & 3 & -1 \end{array}\right] .\]

    Compute \(T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 3 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] \right)\).

Answer:

  1. \[ \left[\begin{array}{cc} 3 & 5 \\ -1 & -4 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right] \]

  2. \[T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 3 \\ 7 \end{array}\right] \right)= \left[\begin{array}{c} 27 \\ -18 \\ 43 \\ 6 \end{array}\right] \]
