1. Find the standard matrix for the linear transformation \(S:\mathbb{R}^ 3 \to \mathbb{R}^ 3 \) given by

    \[S\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{array}\right] \right) = \left[\begin{array}{c} x_{1} - x_{2} \\ -4 \, x_{1} + 5 \, x_{2} - 4 \, x_{3} \\ -4 \, x_{1} + 3 \, x_{2} + 5 \, x_{3} \end{array}\right] .\]

  2. Let \(T:\mathbb{R}^ 4 \to \mathbb{R}^ 2 \) be the linear transformation given by the standard matrix

    \[ \left[\begin{array}{cccc} -1 & -6 & 6 & -4 \\ 1 & 5 & -5 & 3 \end{array}\right] .\]

    Compute \(T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] \right)\).

Answer:

  1. \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & -1 & 0 \\ -4 & 5 & -4 \\ -4 & 3 & 5 \end{array}\right] \]

  2. \[T\left( \left[\begin{array}{c} 7 \\ 3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] \right)= \left[\begin{array}{c} -55 \\ 46 \end{array}\right] \]
