Show how to find the solution set for the following system of linear equations.

\begin{alignat*}{4} 2 \, x_{1} & & &-& 4 \, x_{3} &=& 2 \\3 \, x_{1} &+& x_{2} &-& 3 \, x_{3} &=& 0 \\3 \, x_{1} &+& x_{2} &-& 3 \, x_{3} &=& 0 \\x_{1} & & &-& 2 \, x_{3} &=& 1 \\ \end{alignat*}

Answer:

\[\mathrm{RREF} \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 2 & 0 & -4 & 2 \\ 3 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 3 & 1 & -3 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & -2 & 1 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 3 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right] \]

The solution set is \( \left\{ \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \, a + 1 \\ -3 \, a - 3 \\ a \end{array}\right] \middle|\,a\in\mathbb{R}\right\} \)
