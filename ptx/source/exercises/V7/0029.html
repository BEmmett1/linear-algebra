Consider the subspace

\[W=\operatorname{span} \left\{ \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} 8 \\ -4 \\ -2 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -22 \\ 6 \\ 9 \\ 9 \end{array}\right] \right\} .\]

  1. Explain how to find a basis of \(W\).
  2. Explain how to find the dimension of \(W\).

Answer:

\[\operatorname{RREF} \left[\begin{array}{cccccc} 0 & -2 & -2 & 8 & -3 & -22 \\ -4 & -2 & 2 & -4 & 1 & 6 \\ 1 & 2 & -2 & -2 & 2 & 9 \\ -1 & 0 & 2 & -4 & 1 & 9 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{cccccc} 1 & 0 & 0 & 2 & -1 & -5 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -3 & \frac{3}{2} & 9 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & -1 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right] \]

  1. A basis of \(W\) is \( \left\{ \left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -4 \\ 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -2 \\ 2 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ 2 \\ -2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] \right\} \).
  2. The dimension of \(W\) is \( 3 \).
