Explain how to find the eigenvalues of the matrix \(\left[\begin{array}{cc} -2 & -2 \\ 10 & 7 \end{array}\right] \text{.}\) \end{problem} \â€‹begin{solution} Compute the characteristic polynomial:
\begin{equation*} \det(A-\lambda I) = \det \left[\begin{array}{cc} -2 - \lambda & -2 \\ 10 & 7-\lambda \end{array}\right] = (-2-\lambda)(7-\lambda)+20 = \lambda ^2 -5\lambda +6 = (\lambda -2)(\lambda -3) \end{equation*}
The eigenvalues are the roots of the characteristic polynomial, namely \(2\) and \(3\text{.}\)
